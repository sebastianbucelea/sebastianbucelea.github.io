The members

Jose Armando Henriques Roa

Ever since the age of 4 when I was handed a GameBoy Advance I have spent most of my waking life submerged in the console side of the video game universe. That is until 6 years ago when I was finally able to expand my horizons to the PC world. Ever since then I have introduced myself to a broad array of brand new game styles, and amongst them all, the ones that has stuck with me the most are the ”Indie” games. I had thought after all those credits at the end of each game that video games could only be made by teams of no less than 50 people. And never would have thought two of my favorite games ”Don’t Starve” and ”Stardew Valley” could have been created by no more than 2. Needless to say the intent creating my own game is to this day the main reason for my drive to hone my programming skills.

Sebastian Bucelea

My first encounter with programming took place in the 9th grade when I had my first C++ courses. Ever since, this domain has represented a passion for me.Despite the fact that I did not have the chance to study it in the following high school years, I have learned it as a hobby, until joining EPITA in order to shape and enhance my knowledge in this domain. Some of the applications of what I have learned have been several basic games in Unity, which have been implemented in C#, more or less different from the ones presented in the tutorials on its website, such as Roll-aBall, which have allowed me to discover the basics of this game engine. Therefore, game development has been to this day one of the domains that I have been keen on, mainly because of the various skills needed, as well as for its positive influence on my programming and designing skills. Being the leader of this group will not only allow me to positively influence and motivate my teammates, but to improve my management skills and my work ethic.
